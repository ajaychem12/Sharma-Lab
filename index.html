Welcome to Sharma Lab

The Sharma Lab at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine is dedicated to advancing the field of molecular imaging and radiopharmaceutical chemistry. Our mission is to develop innovative tools that enhance the diagnosis and treatment of cancer, with a particular focus on pancreatic and ovarian cancers. Led by Dr. Ajay Kumar Sharma, our research combines cutting-edge chemistry with biology to create highly selective molecular probes and targeted radiopharmaceuticals. These agents are designed to improve the precision of cancer imaging and therapy, enabling earlier detection, better monitoring of disease progression, and more effective personalized treatment options.

Research

Our lab focuses on developing innovative molecular tools to improve cancer diagnosis and treatment. We specialize in:

  • Radiopharmaceutical Chemistry: Designing and synthesizing radioactive compounds that precisely target cancer cells for imaging and therapy.
  • Targeted Molecular Imaging: Creating advanced imaging agents that visualize tumors at the molecular level, enabling early detection and personalized treatment monitoring.
  • Peptide-Based Diagnostics and Therapeutics: Engineering peptide molecules that specifically bind cancer biomarkers, serving as powerful platforms for both precise diagnosis and targeted therapy.

Team

Ajay Kumar Sharma, Ph.D. - Principal Investigator

Dr. Ajay Kumar Sharma is a passionate research scientist specializing in molecular imaging and radiopharmaceutical chemistry. He leads the Sharma Lab at Johns Hopkins School of Medicine, where his research focuses on developing cutting-edge tools for precision diagnosis and treatment of cancer. With a strong background in peptide engineering, PET imaging, and targeted therapy, Dr. Sharma’s work bridges chemistry, biology, and translational oncology to improve patient care. He is also a recipient of multiple prestigious grants recognizing his innovative contributions to cancer research.
